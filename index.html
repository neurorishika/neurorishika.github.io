I’m a neuroscientist (in training) in love with insect brains, human social behavior and politics, a roleplay-focused TTRPG storyteller and a proud trans woman.

I have a keen interest in the field of Mechanistic Neuroethology, Sensory Coding and its Evolution, and Neural Mechanisms for Learning. Studying different animals and their sensory systems in the context of their ecology and evolutionary history is something I hope to do during my career in research. I enjoy programming, fabricating new devices, and interdisciplinary approaches to solving traditional problems. I also have a strong interest in humanities and social sciences particularly economics, feminist/queer theory & literary theory, and wish to explore the subject further. In my free time, I moonlight as a Tabletop Roleplaying Storyteller and Moderator for anyone interested. My games are player-focused and lean towards rewarding immersive roleplay to create an creative environment for collective storytelling.

Projects

Air Pollution is a major contributor to both short-term and long-term health problems and can also affect major interactions between different components of the ecosystem such as Plant-pollinator interactions. We studied the effect of Air Pollution, particularly suspended particulate matter on the Giant Honeybee, Apis dorsata. For this objective, different physiological parameters such as Hemolymph Quality, Respirometry, and Morphometry of Adult Apis dorsata foragers were studied and a comparative analysis was done on bees collected from different field sites in the city of Bangalore, India in order to correlate these parameters with levels of PM 10 and PM 2.5 particles in the sites.

Effect of Air Pollution on Apis dorsata (Completed)

NICE Lab, NCBS, Bangalore

Rhagoletis pomonella has been extensively studied as a model system for speciation in progress, particularly incipient sympatric speciation by neural changes in odour processing by causing a behavioural shift. This makes R. pomonella host-choice an important model for studying the neural basis of behaviour and decision-making. Field behaviour assay presents a lot of random variables which are not under control. Thus, the use of Virtual Reality presents a new avenue for research. We try to explore the data obtained from a series of experiments performed in Virtual Reality, try to understand the behaviour and model it.

Modeling Flight Behavior of Rhagoletis pomonella (Apple Maggot Fly) in Virtual Reality (Completed)

NICE Lab, NCBS, Bangalore

The simulation of a network of n neurons is essentially the solving of F(n) simultaneous Differential Equations where F is a function of the network architecture and mathematical model of neurons and synapses. Here, a programming framework was developed in python based on Google’s Tensorflow package for accelerating the integration of neuronal models using specialized parallel compute processors such as Nvidia’s Graphical Processing Units (GPUs) and Tensor Processing Units (TPUs). An open-source Python framework was developed that allows high readability, quick experimentation and scalability from individual systems to GPU clusters.

PSST - Scalable framework for Simulating Biological Neural Networks using Tensorflow. (Completed)

Theoretical Neuroscience Lab, IISER Pune

Drosophila melanogaster can learn to associate rewards or punishments to odour cues. The goal of this project was to carefully characterize how learning alters fly behavior. We designed and set up a Closed-Loop Virtual Reality arena where the fly controls a rotating odour nozzle by walking on a floating ball which we used to test three experimental paradigms: i) behavioural responses to innately attractive and repulsive odours ii) responses to optogenetically stimulating attractive and repulsive signalling MB Output neurons and iii) behavioural responses to flies trained to form Pavlovian associations with odour.

Investigating Odor-based Learning in Closed-Loop Fly-on-Ball VR. (Completed)

Turner Lab, Janelia Research Campus, Ashburn

Previous theoretical studies of the Locust Olfactory System have proposed a model of odor representation via activation of sub-networks of all AL neurons which leads to spatiotemporal patterning. We propose an alternate idea in which the network structure of the AL gives rise to a dynamical system with a large number of attractors and the activity of a sub-network of PNs acts as a director of the activity on odor onset. We build a realistic scaled-down model of the complete locust olfactory system and study the dynamics of the odor representations.

Dynamics of Odor Representations across different layers of Locust Olfactory System. (Ongoing)

Theoretical Neuroscience Lab, IISER Pune

As people of Science, it is essential that we understand any biases or perceived threats to inclusivity, so that we can move to combat inequality and discrimination. The aim of this study is to understand the extent of inclusivity in an emergent intersectional community such as neuroscience (including the AI and cognitive psychology communities), a community known for its interdisciplinarity and diversity of backgrounds. We believe that the Neurosciences community is fertile ground not only to sample from, but to also propel early proactive course-correcting if necessary.

DiverseNeuro.org: A case study on Inclusivity in Neuroscience. (Ongoing)
